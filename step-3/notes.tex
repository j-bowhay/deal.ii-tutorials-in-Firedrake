In this tutorial we will solve the following problem
\begin{align}
    -\nabla^2 u &= 1 \quad \tin \quad \Omega,\\
    u &= 0 \quad \ton \quad \partial\Omega,
\end{align}
where $\Omega=[-1, 1]^2$. We multiply the equation by the test function $v$
which gives
\begin{equation}
    -\int_\Omega v \nabla^2u \dd{x} = \int_\Omega v\dd{x}.
\end{equation}
Integrating by parts, we then find
\begin{equation}
    \int_\Omega \nabla v \cdot \nabla u \dd{x}
    -\int_{\partial\Omega}v\mathbf{n}\cdot\nabla u = \int_\Omega v\dd{x}.
\end{equation}
Finally, by noting the boundary conditions, we end up with
\begin{equation}
    \int_\Omega \nabla v \cdot \nabla u \dd{x}
    = \int_\Omega v\dd{x}.
\end{equation}
